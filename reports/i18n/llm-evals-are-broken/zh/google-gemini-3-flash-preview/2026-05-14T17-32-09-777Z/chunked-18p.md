## Article Summary
This analytical and prescriptive article argues that generic AI benchmarks are irrelevant for most businesses, who instead suffer from "vibes-based evaluation" and silent regressions. The author contends that teams must move away from relying on user complaints or "LLM-as-judge" metrics and instead implement rigorous, task-specific evaluation frameworks. Key strategies discussed include the creation of "Golden Sets" derived from real-world failure cases and the use of deterministic, programmatic checks (e.g., JSON validation, citation grounding) over expensive human or biased model-based grading. The piece adopts a pragmatic, slightly provocative tone, using the metaphor of benchmarks as a "tuxedo" to frame the disconnect between marketing metrics and production reliability. It is intended for software engineers and product teams building LLM-integrated applications.

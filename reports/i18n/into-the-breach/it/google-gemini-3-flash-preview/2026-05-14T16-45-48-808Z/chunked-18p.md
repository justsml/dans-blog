## Article Summary
This analytical article argues that the traditional security model—treating production as dangerous and local development as safe—is obsolete. The author contends that the modern developer laptop has become a "credential warehouse" vulnerable to "boring" but effective attacks like prompt injection, poisoned dependencies, and misconfigured CI/CD workflows. The intended audience is software engineers and DevOps professionals who must shift from a passive "compromised" mindset to an active defense against actions they themselves might trigger. The tone is urgent and pragmatic, framing the developer not just as a victim, but as the primary actor in the modern breach.

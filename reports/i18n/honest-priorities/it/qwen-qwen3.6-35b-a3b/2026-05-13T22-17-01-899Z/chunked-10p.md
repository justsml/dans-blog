## Article Summary
This article argues that rigid, multi-level priority dropdowns in issue-tracking systems like Jira inevitably cause "priority inflation" (e.g., P0, P00), creating maintenance nightmares and misrepresenting the fluid nature of real-world priorities. Targeted at engineering managers, product owners, and Jira administrators, it advocates replacing static lists with dynamic alternatives like open-ended numeric fields, composite scoring, or established frameworks like MoSCoW and the Kano Model. The piece maintains a pragmatic, slightly satirical tone, using recurring metaphors like a "z-index war" to illustrate field inflation and a "Bizzaro Trophy Room" to frame neglected backlogs as graveyards of unshipped work. Ultimately, it emphasizes that sustainable prioritization requires distinguishing priority from severity and enforcing strict backlog hygiene through regular archiving and acceptance of inevitable ticket decay.

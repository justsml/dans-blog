## Article Summary
This article argues that rigid, fixed-priority dropdowns in project management tools like Jira inevitably trigger "priority inflation," forcing teams into endless workarounds like P0 and P00 that fail to capture the fluid, context-dependent nature of real-world priorities. Targeted at engineering managers, product owners, and development teams, it advocates replacing static lists with flexible numeric scales, composite scoring, or dynamic frameworks like MoSCoW and the Kano model, while emphasizing the critical distinction between priority and severity. Framing the issue through the recurring metaphor of a "z-index war" to illustrate chaotic escalation, the author adopts a pragmatic, slightly satirical tone to urge teams toward proactive backlog hygiene and forward-focused prioritization rather than maintaining a stagnant "trophy room" of unshipped tickets.

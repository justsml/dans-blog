## Article Summary
The article argues that AngularJS v1.x can be enjoyable but suffers performance issues in medium-to-large apps due to overuse of `$watch`, `$scope`, and directive nesting. It critiques the "OVER-Angular.JSification" pattern and offers practical solutions: use one-way binding (`::`), limit recursive directives (especially inside `ng-repeat`), and employ native JS/DOM factories for UI fragments. The tone is a constructive tutorial aimed at AngularJS developers, using metaphors like "2-way sword" and the hammer/nail adage. It also recommends Browserify for project organization and briefly compares ReactJS and Polymer as alternatives.
